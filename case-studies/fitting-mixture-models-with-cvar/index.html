CASE STUDY SUMMARYn This case study demonstrates the example of fitting a mixture of normal distributions (to the empirical distribution) with Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR) constraints on the mixture distribution. The initial approximation without constraint was obtained with the Expectation Maximization (EM) algorithm. Further weights of the mixture were corrected to guarantee the minimal CVaR values (estimating fatness of tails). CVaR distance was used to measure closeness of the mixture model to the empirical distribution. Minimization of CVaR distance subject to CVaR constraints on the mixtures weights is a convex optimization problem.
